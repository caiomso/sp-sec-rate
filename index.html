Safety Rating Methodology

The Safety Rating is a 0-to-100 index measuring crime per capita in each precinct. It combines three steps: weighting incidents by severity (homicide weighs more than theft), normalizing by territorial population, and a logarithmic transformation to distribute precincts meaningfully along the scale. A higher score means lower crime per capita.

Range Level Practical Reading
80 – 100Very SafeCrime per capita well below city average
60 – 79SafeIndicators within acceptable levels
40 – 59ModerateAttention recommended; monitor trend
20 – 39CriticalHigh crime per capita; intervention recommended
0 – 19Very CriticalVery high crime per capita; top priority

Caveats · Ratings cover Q1 (Jan–Mar) of each year — seasonal comparisons are controlled, but one quarter is less robust than a full year. · Data reflects registered crimes only. · 46 of 93 precincts use population estimates based on geographic proximity. · The log scale is non-linear: 10 pts at the top mean less than 10 pts in the middle.

Sources: SSP-SP (476k incidents 2024 · 468k incidents 2025 · Q1 2026) · SEADE · Full methodology available in a separate document.
